EXPLAINING CORONAVIRUS STATISTICS FOR SOME COUNTRIES

COVID-19 pandemic has left us sheltered in our homes. Various news media report the ever-increasing trends in the number of cases and more frightening: the number of deaths. The various news media also report fancy terms like exponential trends, flattening the curve and several more buzz-words which can be overwhelming if this is the first time you have heard such terms. It's hard, not to feel panicked. I, personally, have found it difficult to explain to the elderly family members what these terms mean.

Hence, I decided to put together some interactive plots which shows the COVID-19 statistics for some countries of my interest:

1) The United States of America - I am located here presently and currently this is the country with the highest number of confirmed cases.

2) Brazil - In headlines recently, with second highest infections and many controversies.

3) India - A majority of my family lives here and the number of confirmed cases is going up, alarmingly!

4) Italy - I lived and worked here just before the COVID-19 pandemic hit. Italy was the first country in Europe with severe number of COVID-19 cases, but seems to have handled the situation well, for now.

Data source: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19/master/csse_covid_19_data/csse_covid_19_time_series/time_series_covid19_confirmed_global.csv

This website is updated once daily.

Data analysis is done using pandas and plots are generated using plotly and cufflinks.

1. THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA¶

The novel coronavirus became a US household name only in early March when the number of infected patients started to increase. The exponential nature of both total number of cases and total number of deaths graphs is very evident between 15th March and 15th May. The number of cases seemed to drop from September end but the number of cases has surged again. As of today, the US is experiencing its third wave of the total number of cases with more than 250,000 deaths.

(India). Rather it is interesting to note that India with such a high number of infections have controlled the number of deaths.

IS THE CURVE FLATTENED ?¶

A 7 day moving average in the daily new confirmed cases between the 4 countries show that Italy did indeed flatten the curve for a bit (Now you see the flat line, don't you?).

For the United States, the line flattened for a bit (end of May to beginning end of June) but is on the increasing trend again. India shows no signs of flattening while the trends for Brazil indicates that the country might show a dip in daily new confirmed cases soon.
