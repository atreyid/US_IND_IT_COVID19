CORONAVIRUS STATISTICS FOR COUNTRIES OF MY INTEREST

COVID-19 pandemic has left us sheltered in our homes. Various news media report the ever-increasing trends in the number of cases and more frightening: the number of deaths. The various news media also report fancy terms like exponential trends, flattening the curve and several more buzz-words which can be overwhelming if this is the first time you have heard such terms. It's hard, not to feel panicked. I, personally, have found it difficult to explain to the elderly family members what these terms mean.

Hence, I decided to put together some interactive plots which shows the COVID-19 statictics for some countries of my interest: USA (where I am locatedpresently), India(where a majority of my family lives), Italy (where I lived and worked just before the COVID-19 pandemic hit) and Brazil (which has dorned the headlines for COVID-19 news recently)

Data source: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/CSSEGISandData/COVID-19/master/csse_covid_19_data/csse_covid_19_time_series/time_series_covid19_confirmed_global.csv

This website is updated once daily. Last updated: 2020-06-08 at 17:20:22

UNITED STATES¶

The novel coronavirus became a US household name only in early March when the number of infected patients started to increase. The exponential nature of both total number of cases and total number of deaths graphs is very evident between 15th March and 15th May. As of today, US leads the world in the total number of cases with more than 100,000 deaths.

The number cases per day, however, is thankfully on the decline. The steep slopes of the exponential graphs now show a signs of flattening
